Carbon Footprint 101

Your essential primer on greenhouse gas emissions, climate impacts, and the simple everyday actions you can take to make a global difference.

What is a Carbon Footprint?

The Definition

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases—including carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), nitrous oxide (N₂O), and fluorinated gases—emitted directly or indirectly by our daily activities. It covers everything from the electricity powering your home to the fuel burnt transporting the food on your plate.

What is CO₂e?

Different gases trap heat at different intensities. Methane is over 25 times more potent than carbon dioxide. To simplify measuring, we convert all greenhouse emissions into their carbon dioxide equivalent weight: CO₂e. This provides a single unified metric for tracking global impact.

Why it Matters

The Climate Blanket

Greenhouse gases act like a thermal blanket wrapping our planet. The thicker the blanket, the more solar heat stays trapped. This accumulation is causing global average temperatures to rise, accelerating polar ice melts, severe droughts, extreme storms, and shifting ecosystems.

The Target: 2 Tons

To avoid catastrophic global warming and stabilize under the critical 1.5°C threshold set by climate science, the average individual carbon footprint must fall from the current global average down to a sustainable 2 Tons CO₂e/year by 2050.

🌍 Current Global Average: ~4.5 Tons/year

Core Footprint Sources

Transportation

Solo passenger commutes and flights account for roughly 30% of individual emissions.

Home Energy

Heating, cooling, and electricity use represent about 27% of the personal footprint.

Diet & Food

Agriculture and meat processing contribute 20% of emissions, mostly via methane.

Consumption

Purchasing manufactured goods and fast fashion comprises about 23% of your impact.

Everyday Actionable Reductions

Food

Swap dairy and beef meals for plant-based grains or beans.

Saves ~1,200 kg CO₂e/yr

Transport

Car-free or ride sharing commutes to school/work weekly.

Saves ~800 kg CO₂e/yr

Energy

Replace standard incandescent bulbs with efficient LEDs.

Saves ~300 kg CO₂e/yr

Shopping

Avoid fast fashion: purchase durable, secondhand wear.

Saves ~500 kg CO₂e/yr

Myths vs Facts

Myth

Recycling is the best way to reduce my personal carbon footprint.

Fact

While recycling is critical, switching to renewable home utilities and eating less red meat yields significantly higher footprint reductions.

Myth

Electric vehicles have zero carbon footprint during use.

Fact

An EV is only as clean as the power grid supplying its electricity. Charging on solar or wind power maximizes your footprint savings.

Myth

Buying local food always cuts transport emissions down to zero.

Fact

Transport represents a small portion of a meal's footprint. How food is grown and produced (e.g. heated glasshouses) matters much more.

Glossary of Key Terms

Carbon Footprint

The net greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by an entity, individual, or event.

Carbon Neutral

Balancing carbon releases with equivalent offsets so the net increase to global emissions equals zero.

CO₂e (Equivalent)

A metric measure used to compare emissions from various greenhouse gases on the basis of their global warming potential.

Net-Zero

Reducing greenhouse emissions as close to absolute zero as possible, offset only by natural sink removals (like forests).

Carbon Offset

Compensating for carbon releases by funding equivalent emissions reduction projects elsewhere (e.g. planting trees).
